Machine learning (ML) is a field of study in artificial intelligence concerned 
              with the development and study of statistical algorithms that can learn from data and generalize to unseen data, 
              and thus perform tasks without being explicitly programmed.
              Advances in the field of deep learning have allowed neural networks, a class of statistical algorithms, to surpass
               many previous machine learning approaches in performance.

             Statistics and mathematical optimisation methods compose the foundations of machine learning. Data mining is a related field 
              of study, focusing on exploratory data analysis (EDA) through unsupervised learning.

             From a theoretical viewpoint, probably approximately correct learning provides a mathematical and statistical framework 
              for describing machine learning. Most traditional machine learning and deep learning algorithms can be described 
              as empirical risk minimisation under this framework.

              Machine learning is sometimes conflated with data mining, where the latter subfield focuses more on exploratory data analysis 
              and is known as unsupervised learning. Machine learning is also sometimes conflated with predictive analytics,
              where the latter subfield focuses on statistical modelling and is known as supervised learning. 
              Machine learning and predictive analytics often employ the same methods and overlap significantly, but while predictive 
              analytics aims to accurately predict specific outcomes for future data, machine learning focuses on allowing computers to
               learn to generalize from existing data in order to produce accurate predictions for new data.
